Competency 1: Communication

Learning Module 1.2: Importance of Emotional Intelligence


What are interpersonal skills? What personality traits are important in the lodging industry? How do you demonstrate orderly, systematic, and responsible behavior? What are self-understanding, self-esteem, and self-image?
Photo Credit: iStockPhoto/diatrezor


Learn


Module Introduction. Interpersonal skills are the tools people use to interact and communicate with individuals in an organizational environment. Self-concept is an understanding of who you are as a person. Self-concept and communication skills are two the key qualities a lodging industry professional must possess. Other skills that a hospitality professional must acquire are verbal communication, non-verbal communication, listening skills, negotiating, problem solving, precision making and assertiveness.
Learning Targets. Upon the completion of this module you will be able to:

  • Demonstrate such interpersonal skills as punctuality, initiative, courtesy, loyalty, and being a team player.
  • Identify personality traits important to the lodging industry.
  • Demonstrate orderly, systematic, and responsible behavior
  • Explain concepts of self-understanding, self-esteem, and self-image
